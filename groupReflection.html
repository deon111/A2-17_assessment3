Feedback & Group Reflection


Group Reflection

What went well

We delegated tasks early in the assessment based on individual abilities and interests. For example, one group member was able to develop a prototype for the app and another designed the website and created the video as they had the most expertise out of the group in those areas. The checklist assigned group members tasks relating to assignment criteria and marking rubric and set out a specific date that the task was to be completed by. This helped to improve overall organisation from Assessment 2. Better interactive collaboration in the assignment was achieved by group members doing sections together.

There was also a more equal distribution of workload, unlike Assessment 2 where almost half of the Assignment was completed by just one group member. There was more time for feedback which improved the quality of the report during revisions. Team members also had more time to help with the sections they were not assigned to do. More active listening was involved which resulted in better coordination and confidence in doing tasks assigned.

Group members have shown great flexibility in this assignment, as they were able to adjust to difficult situations they encountered. If someone was behind on their work, another member would compensate for them. Furthermore, everyone addressed issues without complaining or showing signs of stress.

What could be improved

Similar to A2, the group suffered from lack of organisation, especially during early stages of production. Only the deadline forced everyone commit more, however this is a stressful situation that could have been avoided. Furthermore, all group should have assisted in problem solving. Problems can be difficult to manage and there were countless problems encountered in this assignment, group members have agreed that there was a lack of solutions offered. In future assignments, the group will endeavor to rectify these mistakes.

Thing that was surprised

A few revisions were needed to adequately finish each section. There was more work involved in writing the report for this assessment. Moreover, a high level of coordination with other group members was necessary to complete this report. All sections were interconnected. There was less coordination during the previous assessment, as the report sections were less connected.

Thing that we learned

Being flexible with tasks assigned or taken and being willing to fill in for other members was important for finishing the project. Having team members that had different strengths and weaknesses allowed for better distribution of tasks. Team members were delegated tasks they were more experienced with. Feedback given for the report was also helpful as there were different points of view.

This was a bigger assignment in scope than Assessment 2 but the group seemed to handle their work more comfortably. There wasn't one whole section still incomplete on the final day as was the case in the first group assignment.
